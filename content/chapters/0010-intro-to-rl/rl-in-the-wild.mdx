<Intuition>

**RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)** is how modern LLMs learn to be helpful:

**RLHF Definition:** RLHF is a specific technique that is used in training AI systems to act more human, along with other techniques such as supervised and unsupervised learning. There is a fact that RLHF makes the ML model more accurate. Models can be trained on pre-generated human data, but having additional human feedback loops significantly enhances model performance compared to its initial state.

**How does RLHF work?**

RLHF is performed in 4 stages before a model is ready:

**1. Data Collection:**¬†Gather a large set of human-generated prompts and responses to help the model learn language patterns.

**2. Supervised Fine-Tuning:**¬†Start with a pretrained model and fine-tune it on your company‚Äôs knowledge, for example using¬†retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). Compare the model‚Äôs answers to human responses and score them (e.g., 0‚Äì1) based on how well they match. The model learns to prefer higher-scoring responses, guiding future answers.

**3. Build a Reward Model:**¬†Train a separate reward model from human feedback. Humans rank multiple responses to the same prompt, and the reward model learns to predict which responses humans would prefer.

**4. Optimize the Model with RL:**¬†The language model uses the reward model to refine its policy, evaluating multiple responses and choosing the one most likely to satisfy human preferences.

**Example:** In autonomous vehicles, engineers rank driving behaviors (smooth braking vs. aggressive lane changes). The reward model predicts human preferences, and RL fine-tuning trains the car to choose actions humans trust, ensuring safer and more comfortable driving

**In short**, RLHF ensures that autonomous vehicles don‚Äôt just drive by simply following the rules, they aim to drive¬†in a way humans actually like and trust.

</Intuition>

<Note title="Why RL for LLMs?">

Supervised learning alone can't optimize for "helpfulness" or "honesty"‚Äîthese are subjective qualities that emerge from human judgment. RL bridges this gap by learning directly from human preferences, not just text examples.

</Note>

## The Pattern: When to Think "RL"

<Tip title="RL Might Be Right When...">

Ask yourself these questions about your problem:

1. **Sequential decisions?** ‚Äî Actions now affect future options
2. **Delayed feedback?** ‚Äî You don't know immediately if a choice was good
3. **No labeled "correct" answers?** ‚Äî Just outcomes to evaluate
4. **Need to balance exploration?** ‚Äî Unknown options might be better

If you answered yes to most of these, you might have an RL problem.

</Tip>
